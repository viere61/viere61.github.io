Matter

Matter

Matter both

  • Occupies space
  • Has mass

Matter is Comprised of Atoms

For example:

Gold is an element (a substance of atoms having an identical number of protons in each nucleus)

Dividing a gold coin into smaller and smaller pieces might eventually yield a single gold atom

If we devide that single atom, it will no longer be gold.

Most elements are unstable and react. Only a few elements exist commonly in nature as independent single atoms.

For example: He, Ne, Ar

Those are called the inert gasses, or the noble gasses.

Molecules and Compounds

Atoms combine to form molecules

Case 1:

2 or more identical atoms

  • $N_2$ is a molecule and an element
  • $C_60$ is a molecule and an element

Case 2:

2 or more different types of atoms

  • $N_2O$ is a molecule and a compound
  • $H_2O$ is a molecule and a compound

Definition of compound: 2 or more elements chemically bonded together

Case 3:

not a molecule

  • $N$ is an atom and an element

Mixtures

Contains more than one element and/or compound that are not chemically bonded together and so retain their individual properties.

For example:

Air = $O_2, N_2, CO_2, etc$

Amalgam = $Ag, Hg, Cu, Sn$

Salt water = $Na, H_2O$

Vodka = $CH_3CH_2OH, H_2O$

Classifying mixtures

  • Homogeneous Mixtures

    Uniform properties

  • Heterogeneous Mixtures

    Non-uniform properties

    Ex: Salt + Sand, Oil + Water

Phases of Matter

Solid, liquid, Gas

Diatomic molecules

The “gens”:

Hydrogen (H), Nitrogen (N), Oxygen (O)

The halogens: F, Cl, Br, I
